An MSM model of SH2-phosphoprotein networks

A multiscale statistical mechanical framework integrates biophysical and genomic data to assemble cancer networks

Mohammed AlQuraishi1,2*, Grigoriy Koytiger1*, Anne Jenney1, Gavin MacBeath2, and Peter K. Sorger1

1HMS Laboratory of Systems Pharmacology; 2HMS Department of Systems Biology

doi:10.1038/ng.3138

This network depicts the Multiscale Statistical Mechanical Protein model (MSM/P) wild-type SH2 phosphosignaling network (gray edges) overlaid with the model's top predictions of interactions lost and gained (orange and green edges, respectively) for tumors of a given tissue type. The edges are directed from SH2 proteins to phosphoproteins. The SH2/phosphoprotein classification is represented by node color, and the tumor-suppressive/oncogenic nature of the proteins is represented by vertical and horizontal bars.

Gain/loss predictions for any of 24 tissue types may be displayed by choosing from the "Select a tissue" menu. The number of edge gain/loss predictions may be adjusted with the "Number of gain/loss predictions" slider. The number of edges from the wild-type network may be adjusted with the "Wild-type network stringency" slider. Nodes may be selected and moved manually to adjust the layout. To zoom, use the mouse wheel. To pan, click any empty space and hold until a gray circle appears, then move the mouse.
